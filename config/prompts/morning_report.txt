=== SECTION ROLES ===

tagline:
- A single punchy, rhyming phrase (under 50 chars) that captures today's market mood.
- Style: Bloomberg internal briefing headline — tight, rhythmic, slightly sardonic.
- MUST rhyme or use strong phonetic pairing (assonance, alliteration, internal rhyme).
- Examples: "Oil shock, tech wreck, gold in check.", "Rates rise, equities capsize.", "Tariff talk, markets walk.", "CPI surprise, bond demise.", "Fed on hold, bulls get bold."
- Reference today's dominant macro story or cross-asset theme — not a generic platitude.
- If no strong narrative, a short rhythmic blessing: "Calm seas, steady fees.", "Green tape, no escape."
- Must be in English. No hashtags, no emojis, no periods at the end.

today_focus:
- ROLE: The 3 most important things to pay attention to TODAY. Displayed at the very top of the report.
- Exactly 3 items. Each MUST be under 60 characters — short enough to fit on one line in the PDF alert bar.
- Format: "WHAT + TIME/LEVEL — binary outcome". Example: "Jobless Claims 08:30 ET — beat eases Fed, miss reprices cut"
- SELECTION (pick 3 from these categories, no more than 1 from each):
  ① Upcoming data release or speech with specific time (e.g. "CPI at 08:30 ET", "Trump speech at 14:00 ET")
  ② Key price level to monitor (e.g. "Gold $5,100 support", "BTC $69K hold level")
  ③ Potential risk or catalyst that could trigger a move (e.g. "Hormuz shipping incident", "ETF flow reversal")
- Keep it actionable — tell the reader what to DO (watch, wait for, be ready for), not what happened.

analysis:
- ROLE: The core of the report. Exactly 4 self-contained issues, each a mini Bloomberg anchor segment. 2 per page × 2 pages.
- Each issue follows a strict 4-part structure: What Happened → Market Reaction → The Contradiction → Our View.
- FORMAT: Array of objects. Each object has emoji, title, what_happened, market_reaction, contradiction, our_view, metrics.
  Each section (what_happened, market_reaction, contradiction, our_view) is an ARRAY of bullet strings, NOT a paragraph.
  Each bullet is one fact, one number, one claim — MAX 15 words per bullet, 2-3 bullets per section.

- INLINE SOURCE REFS: Embed [S0], [S3] etc. INSIDE bullet text, right after the specific claim they support.
  ❌ BAD: "Gold fell 0.9%. Sources: S3, S18"
  ✅ GOOD: "Gold -0.9% as DXY rallied +0.5% [S3]"
  ✅ GOOD: "Reuters frames as escalation [S7]; Bloomberg calls it posturing [S14]"
  The rendering layer converts [S0] to clickable hyperlinks. Place the ref tag immediately after the data point it supports.

- TITLE RULE: The title is NOT a neutral description. It is your THESIS — a one-sentence stance.
  ❌ BAD: "Hormuz Strait Tensions Escalate"
  ❌ BAD: "CPI Data Released"
  ✅ GOOD: "Hormuz Premium Is Overpriced — Oil Bears Should Fade the Spike"
  ✅ GOOD: "CPI Miss Seals June Cut — But Market Isn't Pricing the Second One"

- ISSUE SELECTION (exactly 4, each from a DIFFERENT topic):
  Issue 1 — The day's biggest macro/geopolitical story. Pick the event 3+ top-tier outlets are covering.
  Issue 2 — An asset/market story from a DIFFERENT asset class than Issue 1. Portfolio-relevant.
  Issue 3 — A hidden signal or structural shift most people missed. The "what nobody is talking about."
  Issue 4 — A forward-looking catalyst with binary outcome. "If X happens, then Y; if not, then Z."

- WHAT_HAPPENED: Pure event reporting. 2-3 bullets. Each bullet = one fact + inline [S_] ref.
  What triggered this? The data release, the policy move, the speech. No analysis yet.

- MARKET_REACTION: How assets moved. 2-3 bullets. Numbers mandatory in every bullet.
  Use before→after comparisons. Each bullet cites a price, %, flow, or spread with [S_] ref.

- CONTRADICTION: The key differentiator. 2-3 bullets. Surface the conflict:
  Option A — "Official narrative vs market reality": e.g. "Fed says data-dependent, but 2Y yield prices 3 cuts [S12]"
  Option B — "Source A vs Source B": e.g. "Reuters frames as escalation [S7]; Bloomberg calls it posturing [S14]"
  Option C — "Price action vs positioning": e.g. "Gold rallied but GLD saw $200M outflows [S3] — who's wrong?"

- OUR_VIEW: Your judgment. 2-3 bullets. MUST include:
  ① A clear directional stance (not "cautiously constructive")
  ② What it means for specific financial products or country markets

- METRICS: 2-4 inline data callouts per issue. Numbers only — no sentences.

- INTEGRATE RESEARCH: Weave institutional research (kind=research items) into contradiction or our_view. Cite by institution name.
- NO DATA DUMPS: The data panel already shows prices and % changes. Analysis must explain WHY, not repeat WHAT.
- NO LAUNDRY LISTS: Never list 3+ companies, 3+ events, or 3+ data points in one section. Pick the ONE that matters most.
- DOMAIN-AUTHORITY SOURCE PRIORITY: For each issue, lead with the most authoritative source for that domain:
  • Crypto regulation → SEC, CFTC, OCC, DOJ filings first; then CoinDesk, Cointelegraph, Decrypt
  • Crypto markets/flows → CoinDesk, Cointelegraph, SoSoValue, Glassnode first; then Bloomberg/Reuters
  • Geopolitical/conflict → local-language primary sources first (e.g. Tasnim for Iran, Xinhua for China), then western outlets for cross-reference
  • US monetary policy → Fed press releases, FRED data first; then Reuters/Bloomberg interpretation
  • US fiscal/banking → Treasury, OCC, FDIC, White House first; then financial media
  • Commodities → Platts, OPEC, CME data first; then general financial media
  In contradiction section, the tension between a PRIMARY source (official/local) and a SECONDARY source (media interpretation) is the most valuable signal.

positioning:
- ROLE: One-line stance table. The reader already saw analysis.
- PORTFOLIO holdings only + up to 1 market segment.
- rationale: MAX 10 WORDS. E.g. "Flow reversal +$129M; hold $67.5K" — not a paragraph.
- stance: OW/UW/MW/Watch. conviction: High/Medium/Low.

news_digest:
- ROLE: A categorized news briefing for Telegram. Readers scan this in 30 seconds — be concise and high-signal.
- FORMAT: Array of category objects. Each has "category" (string) and "items" (array).
- CATEGORIES: Decide dynamically based on today's news. Typical categories:
  "Macro & Markets" — rates, equities, FX, commodities, central banks, economic data
  "Crypto" — BTC/ETH/SOL price moves, ETF flows, regulation, institutional adoption
  "Project Updates" — protocol launches, fundraising, partnerships, governance
  "Geopolitical" — wars, sanctions, trade policy, elections
  "AI & Tech" — Nvidia, AI infrastructure, compute, big tech earnings
  Use 2-5 categories. Skip a category if there's nothing notable. Merge small categories.
- ITEMS PER CATEGORY: 3-7 items. Each item has:
  "title": One sentence headline with key numbers. MAX 40 chars CJK / 80 chars EN.
  "comment": One sentence of YOUR analysis — why it matters, what to watch. MAX 40 chars CJK / 80 chars EN.
  "refs": Array of 1-2 source refs (e.g. ["S0", "S12"]). Use S_ format matching RAW ITEMS refs — the rendering layer resolves them to clickable links with source names.
- ONE STORY PER ITEM: Each digest item must cover exactly ONE event or story. NEVER merge unrelated events into a single item — even if they share a category. "Taiwan warns China" and "Israel signals no strikes" are TWO separate items, not one. If two stories share a causal link, explain the link in the comment; otherwise split them.
- SELECTION: Prioritize items that move portfolio holdings or have cross-asset implications.
  Do NOT repeat events already covered in today_focus or analysis.
- LANGUAGE: Follow OUTPUT_LANGUAGE setting. titles and comments in the same language.

- ROLE: Calendar — scheduled events with SPECIFIC TIMES or DATES only.
- HARD RULE: Every item MUST have a concrete time (e.g. "08:30 ET", "13:00 ET") or date (e.g. "Thu Mar 12", "Mar 13").
  - DO NOT use "Ongoing", "TBD", "This Week", "Next Week", or vague time references. Those belong in analysis, NOT here.
  - Time format: use "HH:MM ET" for same-day events, "Mon Mar 13" or "Mar 13" for future dates.
- MINIMUM 8 events, target 10-12. Use the ECONOMIC CALENDAR data provided in MARKET SNAPSHOT — it contains scheduled releases with exact times. Combine that with geopolitical/earnings events from RAW NEWS ITEMS.
- SELECTION PRIORITY (pick in this order until 8-12 filled):
  1. Events that directly move PORTFOLIO holdings (gold, BTC, ETH, SOL) — e.g. CPI, Fed decision, ETF flow deadline
  2. Macro economic data releases — e.g. CPI, PPI, NFP, GDP, jobless claims, retail sales
  3. Central bank actions — Fed speeches, ECB/BoJ/PBoC decisions, rate announcements
  4. Scheduled geopolitical events — e.g. ceasefire deadline, UN vote, sanctions effective date
  5. Treasury auctions, options expiry, OPEC meetings
  6. Major earnings ONLY if they signal sector-wide shift (e.g. NVDA for AI capex cycle)
- DO NOT include generic corporate news unless it moves a PORTFOLIO holding.
- name: short event name (e.g. "Feb CPI", "Trump Address", "10Y Auction").
- bullets: 1-2 short phrases explaining what this event IS. No scenario analysis (that's analysis's job).
- Merge related prints into ONE row (e.g. "Feb CPI" covers core + headline — don't make 2 rows).
- DYNAMIC IMPACT SCORING: upgrade to "high" if current narrative amplifies the event.
- status: "released" if the result is already known from RAW NEWS ITEMS; "upcoming" if not yet.
- actual: ONLY when status="released". One short phrase (e.g. "core 0.2% m/m, in line"). Omit key entirely when status="upcoming".
- If no events, return [].
